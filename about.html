About International Assistance {IA} Viewer

About IA Viewer

The International Assistance viewer is a mapping and discovery application put together by a small group of data and mapping geeks. The application leverages data.gc.ca , a key part of Canada's Action Plan on Open Government to enhance transparency and accountability.

This application was built for submission to the Canadian Open Data Experience {CODE} hackathon (February 28 to March 2, 2014). The goal of the app is to help individuals discover and interact with International Assistance information to get a better understanding of how this spending is distributed globally.

The Data

A collection of Open Data was used to create this application:

  • Statistical Report on International Assistance: Data representing statistics on Canada's assistance disbursed by country, by fiscal year. All 14 open data files (CSVs) were combined and statically summarized for the app. Source.
  • World Borders Dataset: A Spatial dataset (shapefile) of world countries used as the interactive map layer. The dataset is available under a Creative Commons Attribution-Share Alike License. Source.
  • Country Flags Icon Collection: A small icon set of world flags by ISO2 code. We wanted to leverage the ISO2/ISO3 codes provided in the IA layers to show there are many ways to bring different types of data together. A special thanks to Jakob Flierl's GitHub repository (licensed for public use). Source.
